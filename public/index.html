What voters in every seat think about same-sex marriage

Posted August 10, 2017 10:02:32

Polling consistently shows a majority of Australians support same-sex marriage. But opinions vary a lot depending where you live.

A year ago, the ABC's Vote Compass asked Australians whether they agreed or disagreed with the following statement:

Marriage should only be between a man and a woman.

More than 1.2 million Australians completed the survey, and across the country a majority of people indicated their support for same-sex marriage.

External Link: Vote Compass same-sex marriage responses

Yes, this data is 12 months old. But it remains the most detailed nationwide snapshot of voters' views on same-sex marriage. Typical polls don't have a big enough sample size to provide data on a seat-by-seat basis.

Also, recent evidence suggests Australian opinion on same-sex marriage is only moving in one direction.

Let's take a closer look at how support for same-sex marriage varies across the nation.
